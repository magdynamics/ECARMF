# MAG Audit Outcome, Resolution, and Value Engine

## Controlled chain

```text
Observation -> validated finding -> professional conclusion -> approved recommendation
-> assigned action -> completion evidence -> verified resolution -> verified outcome
-> approved deliverable -> follow-up and governed learning candidate
```

Every object is versioned and connected to the client, taxpayer, case, jurisdiction,
tax period, issue, evidence, sources, rule version, staff owner, reviewer, and decision.

## Finding controls

A finding requires a facts snapshot, evidence, source versions, rule version,
jurisdiction, confidence, professional conclusion, and reviewer. Machine observations
cannot be released as professional conclusions.

## Recommendation and action controls

Recommendations include alternatives, expected benefit, risks, owner, priority, and
professional approval. Actions include an assignee, reviewer, due date, dependencies,
client decision when required, and completion evidence.

## Resolution and outcome

A resolution records actions performed, taxpayer and authority positions, final
position, evidence, verifier, and date. An outcome records the baseline, method,
evidence, attribution, remaining risk, and professional verification. Financial value
also requires explicit double-counting review.

## Deliverables

Client, internal, management, and tax-authority deliverables have separate audiences
and release controls. Tax-authority deliverables require an approved external action.
Privileged content is prohibited from external deliverables.

## Learning

Outcomes may create learning candidates but never change production rules directly.
Cross-client use requires authorization, technical and legal review, validation,
controlled testing, approval, monitoring, and rollback.
