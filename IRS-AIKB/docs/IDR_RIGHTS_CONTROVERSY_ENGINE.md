# IDR, Taxpayer-Rights, and Controversy Engine

This engine converts Chief Audit Officer findings into internal examination-readiness
workpapers and draft document requests. Each request is limited to the identified
matter, taxpayer, period, and issue. It remains a draft until CPA approval and a
privilege review are completed; the system never transmits it automatically.

For every issue it creates an open workpaper containing the observed fact, authority,
audit technique, evidence request, reasonable explanations to test, preparer field,
reviewer field, and conclusion status. Document receipts are tracked against the
request without treating a partial response as complete.

The workflow includes all ten IRS Taxpayer Bill of Rights protections and control
gates for engagement authority, Form 2848/8821 scope, statute verification, privilege,
preservation, and secure transmission. Deadlines are read from the supplied notice;
the engine does not invent them. Every deadline requires independent human
verification. A deficiency notice triggers immediate controversy-counsel escalation.

The Appeals component produces only a controlled protest outline: disputed changes,
facts, law, requested resolution, evidence index, and a placeholder for the applicable
declaration. Exact deadlines, declaration language, authority, and filing method must
be verified against the notice and current IRS guidance before signing or submission.

Official source foundation:

- IRS Taxpayer Bill of Rights
- Publication 1, *Your Rights as a Taxpayer*
- Publication 5, *Your Appeal Rights and How To Prepare a Protest*
- Publication 556, *Examination of Returns, Appeal Rights, and Claims for Refund*
- IRS, *Preparing a Request for Appeals*

The IRS states that taxpayers have rights to challenge a position, be heard, retain
representation, receive finality, and pursue an independent administrative appeal in
many matters. Availability and deadlines remain matter-specific.
