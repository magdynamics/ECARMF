# Client Transparency and AI Engagement

## Objective

MAG Audit keeps the client informed from onboarding through final outcome. Updates
must reduce uncertainty without minimizing material risks, overstating progress, or
releasing unreviewed conclusions. Every case has an accountable human contact.

## Client communication promise

Each released update answers six questions:

1. What changed?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What work has MAG Audit completed?
4. What happens next?
5. Does the client need to act?
6. Who can answer questions?

The client chooses a permitted channel, language, ordinary update cadence, urgent
channel, and accessibility needs. Material events can trigger an update sooner than
the routine cadence.

## Required engagement journeys

### Service agreement

Approved template -> scope and fee review -> secure delivery -> client questions ->
electronic signature -> evidence receipt -> effective engagement -> supersession log.

### IRS authorization

Representation need -> Form 2848 or information-only Form 8821 decision -> taxpayer,
form, matter, and period validation -> professional review -> secure signature ->
representative declaration when applicable -> IRS submission -> CAF/acceptance tracking.

Joint taxpayers and authorization limits require individual professional review. AI
may prepare data and reminders but may not sign or expand authorization scope.

### Routine case update

Case event -> AI plain-language draft -> confidentiality and quality check -> required
professional approval -> client delivery -> acknowledgment/question routing -> immutable
communication event.

## AI participation

Specialized agents contribute to agreements, authorization, evidence requests,
scheduling, audit-program execution, rights/options, value reporting, and communication
quality. Each works under a versioned contract with allowed inputs/actions, prohibited
actions, approved sources, confidence rules, escalation policy, and human approver.

AI cannot sign, make taxpayer elections, release privileged material, state an
unverified deadline as controlling, send a sensitive conclusion without approval, or
claim financial value without a reviewed baseline, method, evidence, and attribution.

## Client portal

The case view includes:

```text
Overview | Messages | Documents | Signatures | Action items | Progress | Outcome
```

The overview shows the responsible contact, current stage, work completed, next step,
client actions, verified dates, and last update. Internal risk scores, fraud indicators,
privileged analysis, and draft professional conclusions are not exposed automatically.

## Value-driven reporting

Progress and value are reported separately. Progress may include documents reconciled,
issues reviewed, responses prepared, and deadlines met. Outcome value requires a
documented baseline, calculation method, evidence, attribution, professional approval,
and double-counting control.
