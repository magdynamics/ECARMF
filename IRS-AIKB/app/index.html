Retail examination overview

Professional review required

1. Applicable techniques

Reconcile the return to books and bank deposits; test gross receipts from registers, POS and merchant processors; reconcile inventory and cost of goods sold; analyze purchases, shrinkage, markups and gross margin; test cash handling, gift cards, returns, allowances and owner withdrawals.

Sources: IRS Retail Industry Audit Technique Guide; IRM 4.10.3 and 4.10.4. ATGs are examination aids, not controlling law.

2. Gross-profit percentage

Case calculation: ($2,480,000 sales − $1,612,000 COGS) ÷ $2,480,000 = 35.0%.

Prior year: 38.6% · change: −3.6 points. The system flags the change for explanation; it does not assert a universal acceptable retail GP%. Comparisons must match the retail segment, accounting method and period.

Inputs: Demonstration 2024 return and normalized books · reconciliation 96% · calculation confidence 82%.

3. Taxpayer rights

The taxpayer has rights to be informed, quality service, pay no more than the correct tax, challenge the IRS position and be heard, appeal in an independent forum, finality, privacy, confidentiality, representation, and a fair and just tax system.

For this case: verify Form 2848 scope, request clear IDRs and reasonable response time, preserve the right to provide facts and explanations, document contacts, and evaluate manager, Appeals or Advocate escalation when applicable.

Sources: Taxpayer Bill of Rights; IRS Publication 1; applicable examination and Appeals procedures. Rights/options depend on the actual notice and stage.
