# ECARMF Go-to-Market — The 7-Day Strike Plan

*Director of AI · July 2026. Not a 90-day plan. Food on the table this week.*

## The sharpest fact

**The pipeline is already in our database.** Ten real business patterns are fully onboarded —
data models built, risks registered, dashboards live, AI answering. They were called "test
tenants"; they are actually **completed pilots waiting for a price**. Every one of them skipped
the sales cycle, the onboarding project, and the trust-building phase — because it already happened.

## The 7 days

**Day 1–2 (MAG):** Pick the three warmest names. One call each, one sentence:
*"The system you saw me building — your business is already inside it, live. I want to show you
your own risk board for 20 minutes."* Demo THEIR data, not a demo twin. Issue their key on the call.

**Day 2–3 (Director):** Pilot terms ready before the calls end (see PILOT-OFFER.md).
Founding-client pricing: real money, small enough to be a yes on the spot.

**Day 3–5 (MAG + Director):** First invoices out. Every objection comes back to me same-day
with a materials fix or a product fix.

**Day 5–7 (Director):** Whatever each client asks for becomes a package refinement within 48
hours — because **every client is a new dataset and a new skill**; the product compounds with
each sale, which is exactly why speed beats polish.

**Parallel, continuous (Director):** cold-channel materials live (ONE-PAGER, OUTREACH) so warm
momentum feeds cold outreach the moment the first invoice clears.

## Why now — the market window, from live recon (July 2026)

- A wave of AI point-tools is flooding dental/medical operations (scribes, imaging, claims —
  Pearl, Overjet, DentScribe, Archy). **None of them are the governance layer.**
- Platform plays are emerging (Planet DDS "DentalOS" markets a platform-level AI layer for
  DSOs) — the "operating system" language is entering the market. The window to own
  "**governed** AI OS" is open but measured in months, not years.
- The market is saying our thesis out loud: industry guidance now warns that *"AI tools turn
  from productivity tools into compliance liabilities"* and that every AI vendor with PHI access
  is a third-party risk. **Their fear is our product.** Our on-premises model (llama3.1, data
  never leaves the building) is the sentence no cloud copilot can say.

## The kill-shot differentiators (say these, always)

1. **"AI recommends, humans decide — enforced by the software, not the vendor's promise."**
2. **"The AI runs in your building. Patient data never leaves."** (local model, no API key)
3. **"Every entity you own, separated by construction"** — Chase feeds Oak Lawn, BOA feeds
   Elgin, HR policy reaches everyone; misfiled data is *impossible*, not just unlikely.
4. **"Onboarding is authorship, not a project"** — a new industry is a knowledge package,
   which is why we can afford founding-client pricing.

## Accountability

Director owns: every artifact, demo script, pricing model, objection response, package
refinement, and the honest weekly scorecard. MAG/team own: the calls, the sends, the
signatures — AI produces, humans publish. Score: **first invoice ≤ 7 days; three ≤ 30.**
