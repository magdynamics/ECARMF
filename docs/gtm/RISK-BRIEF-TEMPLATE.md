# The Risk Exposure Brief — our opening move

*The anti-telemarketing. We don't ask for their time; we hand them something already worth it.
One page, their name on it, built entirely from public record. The brief IS the marketing.*

---

## Template (fill from public data before any contact)

> # Risk Exposure Brief — {BUSINESS NAME}
> *Prepared {date} · Sources: public records only (City of {city} inspection data{, HHS OCR
> breach portal}{, OSHA enforcement records}) · Prepared by MagDynamics — ECARMF Platform*
>
> ## What the public record shows
> | Date | Location | Event | Status |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | {date} | {address} | Failed health inspection — {violation summary} | Public |
> | {date} | {address} | {repeat violation} | Public |
>
> ## Why this pattern matters
> {N} events across {M} locations in {window} is not bad luck at one site — it is a **control
> gap between locations**: what one location knows, the others don't. That's a management
> visibility problem, and it is fully fixable.
>
> ## What this exposure costs (beyond the fine)
> - Re-inspection fees and the risk of license action on the next repeat
> - This record is visible to your customers, your landlord, and your insurer today
> - The pattern predicts the NEXT violation — inspectors return to repeat offenders sooner
>
> ## What "ahead of it" looks like
> One governed board across all {M} locations: every location's compliance tasks, vendor
> lapses, cash anomalies, and inspection-readiness — scored daily, alarmed before the
> inspector arrives, with an AI analyst (running on-premises, your data never leaves) that
> explains every number. Multi-location operators like yours are our exact specialty.
>
> *This brief is complimentary and yours to keep, whatever you decide.*

## The approach message that carries it

**Email subject: {Business name} — your public risk record ({N} events, {M} locations)**

> {Name} — I run a Chicago platform company, and part of my work is reading public
> enforcement records. Your name came up: {N} failed inspections across {M} locations since
> {month}. That's public — visible to anyone who looks, including customers and insurers.
>
> Rather than ask for your time, I had my team prepare a one-page **Risk Exposure Brief** on
> {business name} — what the record shows, what it costs, and how multi-location operators
> get ahead of the pattern. It's complimentary, no strings, yours either way.
>
> Want me to send it over? — {MAG}, MagDynamics

**Why this works:** it leads with *their* facts, not our product · it gives before it asks ·
the ask is microscopic ("want the brief?") · and answering "yes" starts the opportunity.
The demo, when it comes, opens on a tenant shaped like their business — locations, feeds,
inspection-readiness board — so they see *themselves*, not software.

## The JJ Fish version (warm + documented — call, don't email)

> "Before anything else — did you know your April inspections are on the city's public site?
> Anyone can see them. Here's the thing: the system I built already has your three locations
> in it, and it would have flagged those gaps *before* the inspector walked in. Twenty
> minutes. Let me show you your own board."

## Production rule

Every brief is reviewed by a human (MAG) before it leaves the building — accuracy is the whole
game; one wrong fact destroys the credibility that makes this approach work. Claude drafts
from the data, MAG verifies the events are really theirs (name collisions happen), MAG sends.
