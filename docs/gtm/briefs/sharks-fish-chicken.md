## The pattern that matters
This is the clearest case there is: the *same class* of control failures — missing
certifications, missing health policies, missing supplies — repeating across **five separate
sites**. No single manager is the problem. It's that **each location is running blind to the
others** — there is no shared board telling headquarters which site is about to fail next.
That gap is precisely what our platform was built to close.

## What it costs beyond the citation
- Five public failures compound into a reputation and insurance story, not just fines
- Certification and health-policy gaps are the *easiest* things to prevent — and the most
  embarrassing to fail on, because they're paperwork, not cooking
- With five sites already cited, the city now watches this brand more closely

## What "ahead of it" looks like
One governed board across all five locations: every certificate and training record tracked
with expiry alarms, a per-site readiness checklist, and an on-premises AI analyst that ranks
"which of my five locations is most exposed this week" — daily, before the inspector decides.
Five locations is not a burden for this system; it's the exact reason it exists.
